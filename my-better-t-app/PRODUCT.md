# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who save more than they consume: researchers, knowledge workers, students, and curious learners who have hoarded research papers, browser bookmarks, PDFs, and YouTube watch-later queues they will never realistically read. They already spend daily attention inside TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. Better Scroll meets them in that exact mode of attention rather than asking them to switch into "study mode".

The job to be done is passive consumption of their own saved knowledge during the moments they would otherwise spend on entertainment short-form video. Success is the user finishing a daily drop of reels and feeling they have absorbed something from their backlog without effort or guilt.

## Product Purpose

Better Scroll is a short-form video app where AI-generated reels, derived from the user's own saved sources, are mixed into a stream that feels native to existing short-form platforms. Agents run overnight, scrape and summarize sources, generate scripts, and produce a small finite set of clips (MVP target: ~10 reels per day, 24-30s each, generated via Veo3) ready in the morning.

The product exists because saved-and-never-read is the universal failure mode of bookmarks, papers, and watch-later queues. Real-time on-demand generation is not the goal; pre-generated, finite, daily delivery is. Success looks like: the user opens the app in the same reflexive moment they would have opened TikTok, scrolls through their drop, and learns by accident.

## Brand Personality

Bold, kinetic, addictive. The product borrows the visual language of TikTok, Reels, and Shorts on purpose: full-bleed portrait video, high-contrast type, dark canvas, saturated accents, motion-forward interaction. Knowledge content is delivered in this register, never softened into "educational app" chrome.

In three words: **bold, kinetic, native**. The voice in UI copy is direct and confident, never instructional, never congratulatory. No "Great job!", no streak gamification, no "You're learning!" affirmations.

## Anti-references

- **EdTech and study apps.** Duolingo greens and mascots, Khan Academy gray chrome, Coursera card grids, anything that signals "course platform" or "study tool". Better Scroll is not learning software.
- **Generic SaaS dashboards.** Sidebar + topbar + cards layouts, navy-and-cyan palettes, hero-metric stat blocks. The reel feed is the product, not an admin panel.
- **AI-slop visual cliches.** Purple-to-blue gradients, generic neural-net iconography, "✨ AI-powered" badges, gradient-clipped headline text. The fact that agents power the pipeline should be invisible at the surface.
- **Reading apps.** Pocket, Instapaper, Readwise long-form layouts. Better Scroll is not a reader; if a user wants to read, they would have already.
- **Self-improvement framing.** Streaks, XP, "knowledge journey" progress bars, completion percentages. The product respects the user's existing scrolling habit; it does not try to civilize it.

## Design Principles

1. **Native to the stream.** The reel surface should feel indistinguishable in posture from TikTok, Reels, and Shorts: portrait full-bleed canvas, vertical swipe, minimal chrome. Familiarity is the on-ramp; deviation pays a tax.
2. **Knowledge by stealth.** Substance arrives without effort or self-improvement framing. No congratulatory copy, no progress meters, no "you learned X today" summaries.
3. **The reel is the product.** Feed playback is the only surface that needs to feel exceptional. Sources, settings, and ingestion flows are utility; they should be quiet, fast, and out of the way.
4. **Finite by design.** A daily drop of ~10 reels is a feature, not a limitation. The UI should make the end of the drop feel intentional, not like a feed that broke.
5. **Mobile-shaped, even on web.** The web app is the primary surface, but the canvas is portrait and the interaction model is touch-first. The web build does not earn extra horizontal real estate; it inherits the mobile shape.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Deferred for the MVP demo. Revisit after the reel pipeline and feed are functional. Captions, reduced-motion handling, and contrast audits are expected post-MVP work, not pre-MVP blockers.
